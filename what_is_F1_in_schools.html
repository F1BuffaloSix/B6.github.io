F1 in Schools: Global STEM Racing Competition

What is F1 in Schools?

F1 in Schools is the world's premier STEM educational competition that challenges high school students to design, build, and race miniature Formula 1 racing cars powered by COâ‚‚. This global initiative connects students with the exciting world of motorsport engineering and professional racing.

Teams collaborate using advanced CAD/CAM software to design, analyze, manufacture, and race their miniature compressed air-powered F1 cars. This international platform not only promotes Formula 1 but also develops essential skills in leadership, teamwork, problem-solving, and project management that prepare students for future careers in engineering and technology.

F1 in Schools Mission & Vision

The mission of F1 in Schools is to transform perceptions of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) by creating a dynamic, hands-on learning environment. We aim to inspire students worldwide and provide informed perspectives on careers in engineering, Formula 1, science, marketing, and technology industries.


Our Vision for STEM Education

We envision a future where students are empowered to pursue STEM-related fields with enthusiasm and confidence. By harnessing the excitement of Formula 1 racing, we create opportunities for personal growth and professional development that transcend geographical boundaries and prepare the next generation of engineers and innovators.

F1 in Schools Mexico Program

In Mexico, F1 in Schools is implemented by Persiste A.C., an organization dedicated to promoting STEM education and project-based learning. Since 2015, more than 400 schools across 19 Mexican states have participated in this international racing competition, benefiting over 3,000 students, including a significant representation of women in STEM. Additionally, more than 400 teachers have developed skills to implement F1 in Schools through our comprehensive digital platform.

Join thousands of students worldwide in this exciting Formula 1 educational challenge. For more information, visit the official F1 in Schools website.

Benefits of Participating in F1 in Schools

Skills Development

  • Engineering and design using professional-grade CAD software
  • Project management and teamwork
  • Marketing, sponsorship, and business planning
  • Public speaking and presentation skills

Competition Stages

Students progress through regional and national competitions before reaching the F1 in Schools World Finals, where they compete against teams from over 40 countries. The competition is judged on car speed, engineering design, project management, marketing strategy, and verbal presentation.
